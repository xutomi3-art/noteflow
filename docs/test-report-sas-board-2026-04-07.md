*   **Primary Purpose:** Trips must enhance or strengthen particular curriculum objectives and further the educational goals of the school.
*   **Limitations:** Trips are supported within the limitations of safety and equity of support and opportunity among similar student groups (e.g., same grades or sections of the same course).
*   **Costs:** Students may be asked to pay all or a portion of the costs of trips.

### 7.807P FIELD TRIPS AND EXCURSIONS PROCEDURES
Specific procedures for implementing these trips include [1]:
*   **Approval:** The Principal must approve school-sponsored trips based on security, safety, and educational value.
*   **Consent:** A parental authorization form and liability waiver must be on file with the school office before a student can participate.
*   **Supervision:** Unless special circumstances warrant otherwise, the target student/chaperone ratio is 10:1, with a minimum of 2 adults on any trip. The Principal may adjust this based on the activity and age levels.
*   **Medical Support:** Trips outside Shanghai or in areas with limited medical services require a nurse.
*   **Transportation:** All students must leave from school to attend field trips.
*   **Dismissal from Trips:** Guardians may take students home directly from the trip site provided instruction time is not missed and the school trip supervisor is notified. Students may not leave with anyone other than a guardian without advance written authorization.
*   **Rules:** All school rules apply to students, faculty, and parents during the trip.

For school-sponsored activity trips approved by the administration or identified in the annual budget, the school will partially subsidize travel expenses. However, the balance of the cost must be paid by the student prior to travel [4].

### Drug Testing and Travel Eligibility
Participation in school-related travel may be restricted for students with drug policy violations:
*   **First Offense:** No school-related overseas or overnight domestic travel is permitted for one calendar year [5].
*   **Second Offense:** No overnight travel on school-sponsored trips will be approved for the duration of the student's enrollment at SAS [5].

### Transportation Safety
The School authorizes the use of school-owned and privately-owned vehicles to transport students when engaged in field trips or extra-curricular activities. ### Q5: How are board members selected? Must they be parents?

**Status**: PASS | **Time**: 10.5s

Based on the provided documents, board members are selected through a combination of **election** and **appointment**, and the requirement to be a parent depends on the specific type of board position.

### 1. Selection Process
Board members are selected in two ways:

*   **Election:** Seven members of the Board are elected by the Association (the parents and legal guardians of children registered at the school) [4][6].
*   **Appointment:** Five to seven Board members are appointed by the Board itself [4][6]. When a vacancy occurs for an appointed position, the Board notifies the community and calls for nominations. A **Candidate Vetting Group** (composed of two sitting Board members and 6–8 parents) reviews the candidates and passes their names to the Board, which makes the final selection [5].

### 2. Parent/Guardian Requirement
The requirement to be a parent (a member of the Association) varies by position type:

*   **Elected Members:** **Yes, they must be parents.** All candidates for election must be members of the Association [4][6].
*   **Appointed Members:** **No, they do not need to be parents.** Appointed Board members "may not necessarily be members of the Association" [4][6]. If an appointed member is replaced, the new member "need not be a member of the Association" [5].
*   **Consul General:** **No.** The Consul General of the United States resident in Shanghai (or their designated representative) serves as a member of the Board regardless of parental status [4][6].

### 3. Eligibility Restrictions
The documents also specify who is **not** eligible for membership:
*   A paid employee of the School, or the immediate family member of a paid school employee, is not eligible [6].
*   Spouses of Board members are not eligible [6].

### Q45: SAS的教职工薪酬福利政策是什么？

**Status**: PASS | **Time**: 22.4s

根据提供的文档，上海美国学校（SAS）的教职工薪酬福利政策旨在吸引、激励和留住高素质人才，确保薪酬在区域内具有竞争力，并体现公平和一致性。以下是该政策的主要内容：

### 1. 薪酬福利审查与目标
SAS每年会审查员工的薪酬和福利方案，审查的主要目标包括 [9][10]：
*   确保SAS的薪酬包在区域和本地市场上保持竞争力和良好的定位。
*   让员工感到被重视且薪酬公平，从而激励他们发挥最高水平。
*   在所有职位中体现公平、公正和一致性。
*   提供学习和成长发展的机会。
*   与学校的核心使命、政策和信念保持一致。

### 2. 薪酬确定与批准流程
*   **决定因素**：薪酬是通过分析职位职责、责任、教育背景、经验以及职位成功所需的知识来确定的。每个职位都会与竞争市场进行比较，以评估适当的薪酬水平 [9][10]。
*   **批准流程**：作为年度预算制定过程的一部分，校长将向SAS董事会推荐所有员工的薪酬和福利方案，由董事会批准 [9][10]。
*   **合同期限**：
    *   **海外雇佣员工**：合同包含在初始受雇的前两年中 [9][10]。
    *   **本地雇佣外籍员工**：合同包含在初始受雇的一年中，或任何海外雇佣的延期中 [9][10]。
    *   合同期限通常从8月1日延续至次年7月31日 [9][10]。

### 3. 不同类别员工的福利政策

#### **海外雇佣员工**
*   SAS会向其招聘来校工作的海外雇佣员工提供支持性福利 [9][10]。
*   **生活状态变更**：如果员工的生活状况发生变化（例如已婚夫妇被聘用后决定分居，或作为夫妇共同聘用的一方决定停止在学校工作），学校没有义务续签合同或重复提供现有合同中的福利（如住房规定）[9][10]。

#### **本地雇佣外籍员工**
*   由于学校不招聘本地雇佣员工来上海工作，因此他们的合同不包括住房、交通等福利 [9][10]。
*   除非通过配偶在上海的合同提供，否则将包括健康保险等薪酬和福利 [9][10]。
*   如果配偶的雇主不提供基本福利，SAS保留在员工合同中增加特定福利的权利，但员工必须提供配偶合同中不存在该等福利的证明 [9][10]。
*   该类别包括本地聘用的教师、助教和支持人员 [9][10]。

#### **中国籍员工**
*   中国籍员工的福利和就业条件受《中国劳动法》管辖，并在《中国员工手册》中概述 [9][10]。

### 4. 薪酬发放原则
*   学校将维护透明的薪酬结构，详细说明员工的定期工资、加班费、报销或任何其他应付款项 [9]。
*   **海外雇佣员工薪资**：学校将分不少于十二（12）个等额分期支付工资，首笔支付不晚于8月的最后一个工作日。支付货币为人民币，但在学校 discretion（酌情权）下，年度基本工资的一定百分比可按财务处设定的月汇率以美元、欧元、日元、港币、英镑、澳元、新西兰元、新加坡元或加元支付 [9]。

### 5. 额外职责与补偿
*   教师和助教被要求支持学校的课后计划，承担涉及在标准工作时间和工作日之外与学生接触的课外职责（如担任教练或课外活动的指导老师）[9]。
*   学校将每年为这些课外职责制定津贴，并通过单独的书面协议正式确定付款 [9]。

### 6. 薪酬哲学建议（会议记录中提及）
在会议记录中还提到了一些薪酬哲学的建议原则，旨在优化项目，包括 [10]：
*   年度总薪酬增长应近似于综合消费者物价指数（CPI）的同比变化。
*   采用“自助餐式”模型，汇总外籍人士的旅行、住房和健康福利，以实现更大的员工公平性和对个人福利的控制。
*   考虑基于绩效的奖金或激励措施，以奖励高绩效员工的贡献，而不仅仅基于资历。
